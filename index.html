Welcome to Shakti Milan Samaj

Shakti Milan Samaj (SMS) is an apolitical, non-profit organization based in Kathmandu, Nepal, founded in 1996 by three courageous women who were trafficked, rescued, and living with HIV. United by their shared experiences and determination, they established SMS with a mission to empower women and girls living with HIV/AIDS, survivors of trafficking and victims of gender-based violence through access to adequate care, sustainable advocacy, and community-led transformation.

Our Mission

Ensure gender equality and equity through intersectionality by collaborating with diverse stakeholders and partners to protect the rights and interests of vulnerable women and children living with HIV/AIDS, survivors of trafficking and victims of gender-based violence by the means of ensuring adequate access to healthcare and other relevant services and resources such as education, awareness, direct support, through implementation of sustainable advocacy and relevant strategies to render community awareness, policy changes, institutional reforms - ultimately creating an enabling environment for their empowerment and dignity.

Our Story

Discover the communities we work with and the sustainable solutions we're implementing around the world.

Shakti Milan Samaj (SMS) was born from the courage, pain, and resilience of three women who were trafficked, rescued, and living with HIV. In 1996, in the heart of Kathmandu, they came together not just as survivors, but as changemakers determined to rewrite the story for women like themselves. They had faced the harshest realities: exploitation, stigma, and rejection from both society and their own families. But instead of succumbing to silence, they chose solidarity.

What started as a small, informal support group has now grown into a powerful, apolitical, non-profit organization led by and for women living with HIV/AIDS, survivors of trafficking, and victims of gender-based violence. Our roots lie in lived experience and this remains our strength. We know what it means to be denied dignity, safety, and care, which is why we fight every day to ensure others do not have to walk the same path alone.

Today, SMS stands as a national voice advocating for the rights, health, and empowerment of the most marginalized women and children in Nepal. From providing essential healthcare and psychosocial support, to rescuing trafficked women and advocating for systemic change, our journey continues driven by empathy, resilience, and an unshakable commitment to justice.
